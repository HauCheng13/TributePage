Here's a timeline of Stephen Curry's career

2002-2006 - Curry attended Charlotte Christian School in North Carolina, where he played basketball for the school's varsity team. (Curry was relatively unknown to major college programs because he was smaller-sized)

2006 - As a senior, Curry led his team to a state championship and earned All-state honors. He was highly respected for his incredible shooting ability, ball handling, and scoring ability.

2006 - Curry enrolled at Davidson College, a small school in North Carolina, he played for Davidson Wildcats under coach Bob McKillop.

2006-2007 - As a freshman, Curry had a great season, averaging 21.5 points per game and making a huge impact to his team, even though Davidson was a mid-major school.

2007-2008 - Gained attention nationally by leading Davidson to the Elite Eight in the NCAA tournament, averaging 25.9 points per game.

2008-2009 - Became the nation's leading scoring (28.6 points per game). Declared for the NBA draft.

2009 - Drafted 7th overall by the Golden State Warriors

2014-2015 - Won his first NBA Championship and was named NBA MVP, leading the warriors to a 67-win season.

2015-2016 - Became the first unanimous MVP, setting an NBA record with 402 three-pointers and leading the Warriors to an NBA-best 73 wins.

2017-2018 - Secured back-to-back championships, solidifying the Warriors' dynasty.

2022 - Captured his fourth NBA Championship and won his first NBA Finals MVP.

2024 - Olympic debut in 2024 Paris games, wins the gold Medal with Team USA

Legacy Highlights

4× NBA Champion (2015, 2017, 2018, 2022).

2× MVP (2015, 2016).

10× All-Star and NBA’s all-time leader in three-pointers made.

1x Olympic Gold Medalist.
